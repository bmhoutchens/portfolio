About Me

My Story

My story begins in the south suburbs of Chicago, Illinois. In elementary school, I never had any trouble in my classes, and was a top student. This, coupled with the fact that I was a severe introvert at the time, made me a prime target for some of the other, not so nice students. Eventually highschool came along and I was again, in the top of my class for the majority of my career. During this time, I had my first introduction to programming. We had an elective called Computer Science 101, and decided to join the class. In this class we learned java, and I really enjoyed it. Over the next few years, I took a variety of different programming classes covering several different popular languages. These languages included Python, Small Basic, and App Inventor(a program used to create apps for android devices). During this time I held several jobs, starting with McDonalds at the age of 16. After that, I moved onto Subway, Oberweis, and then The Home Depot, when I moved out to Indiana in the Spring of 2016. After about 2 years of working there, I moved to St. Vincent 86th for a few months as an Inventory Coordinator before going to the Eleven Fifty programming bootcamp. There, I spent twelve weeks learning several new skills to add to my repertoire including HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React, Node.js, and Angular. We also practiced Agile, and partner coding.
